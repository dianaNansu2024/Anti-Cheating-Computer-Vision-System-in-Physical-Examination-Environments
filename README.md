# Anti-Cheating-Computer-Vision-System-in-Physical-Examination-Environments
Examinations are essential for assessing knowledge, awarding credentials, and determining academic progression. However, examination malpractice, particularly cheating during physical exams, remains a serious challenge worldwide. Human invigilators, while effective to some extent, face limitations such as divided attention, fatigue, and difficulty in monitoring large numbers of students simultaneously.
Technological advances in computer vision and artificial intelligence (AI) offer opportunities to strengthen exam monitoring. By automatically detecting suspicious activities in real time, these systems can enhance fairness and preserve academic integrity. This proposal outlines the design of a computer vision-based anti-cheating system focusing on three parameters: head tilt angle detection, hand tracking, and paper/item exchange monitoring.
